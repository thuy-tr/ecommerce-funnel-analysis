# Key Insights

## Funnel Performance

The dataset contains 5,000 users who viewed products.  
Out of these users:

- 1,553 added a product to cart
- 1,103 started checkout
- 899 entered payment information
- 826 completed a purchase

The overall conversion rate from product view to purchase is approximately **16.5%**.

The largest drop-off occurs between **page view and add to cart**, where only about **31%** of users continue to the next step.

This suggests that product pages may not be convincing enough for many users.

---

## Traffic Source Performance

Traffic sources show clear differences in conversion performance.

Email traffic has the **highest conversion rate**, meaning users coming from email campaigns are more likely to complete a purchase.

Organic search brings the **largest number of users**, which results in the highest total revenue even though its conversion rate is lower than email.

Social traffic generates visitors but has the **lowest conversion rate**, suggesting lower purchase intent.

---

## Product Performance

Revenue is not evenly distributed across products.  
A small number of products generate a significant portion of total revenue.

This pattern is common in e-commerce where a few popular items drive most sales.

---

# Recommendations

**Improve product page effectiveness**

Since the largest drop-off occurs before users add products to the cart, improving product pages could significantly increase conversions. Possible improvements include clearer product descriptions, better visuals, and stronger call-to-action elements.

**Invest more in high-performing channels**

Email traffic shows the strongest conversion rate. Expanding email marketing efforts or optimizing lifecycle campaigns could generate more sales.

**Review social traffic quality**

Social traffic converts poorly compared to other channels. It may be useful to review targeting, ad messaging, or landing pages associated with social campaigns.

---

This analysis highlights opportunities to improve both marketing performance and user conversion throughout the purchase funnel.
